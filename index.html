Welcome!

I'm a Biological Sciences PhD at Louisiana State University, where I develop machine learning frameworks to understand complex biological systems at the intersection of computational biology, cheminformatics, and drug discovery.

My research focuses on building data-driven approaches to elucidate bacteria–metabolite interactions in the human gut microbiome, leveraging enzyme encodings and metabolite structural embeddings across various mammalian gut bacterial species. A significant part of my work involves designing data analysis approaches and graph neural network (GNN) models to predict gut bacteria metabolism, synergistic anticancer drug combinations, enhanced by novel data augmentation strategies. I have also created biopython based data curation and cleaning pipelines for to projects related to ligand-binding site classification.

At LSU, I am fortunate to be mentored by Dr. Michal Brylinski. My academic journey includes a BS-MS Dual Degree in Chemistry from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research Bhopal, where I developed cheminformatics tools for toxicity prediction and biofilm-inhibitory molecule classification, and an MS in Chemistry from The Ohio State University where I worked on deep neural network based prediction of newtonian forces on the dipeptide atoms calculated using MD trajectories.

I've also worked as a Research Associate at the Aquatic Germplasm and Genetic Resources Center, where I developed high-performance image classification tools using YOLOv10b with 96% accuracy to classify the fertilized embryos from unfertilized eggs in Xenopus laevis. Currently, I am exploring LLM-based approaches for binding affinity prediction & antibiotic resistance modeling in microbes.

I'm always open to discussing computational biology, new research, or potential collaborations. Feel free to explore my Publications, or connect with me on LinkedIn.
